\noindent\textbf{3.} Suppose not. Let $\algebraicset$ be the set of
all algebraic numbers. Since we assume the opposite any real number is
algebraic and it follows that $\reals \subset \algebraicset$. By exercise
2.2 we know that $\algebraicset$ is countable. Theorem 2.8 shows that
$\reals$ is countable since it is an infinite subset of
$\algebraicset$ which is countable. But this is a contradiction since
Theorem 2.43 shows that $\reals$ is uncountable. Hence our assumption
is false and there exists real numbers $x \in \reals$ such that $x \notin
\algebraicset$.
