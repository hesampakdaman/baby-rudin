\noindent\textbf{17.} We can construct a parallellogram with sides
$\mathbf{|x|, |y|}$ and diagonals $\mathbf{|x+y|, |x-y|}$.  It holds
that
\begin{align*}
  \mathbf{|x+y|}^2 + |\mathbf{x-y}|^2 & = \mathbf{|x|}^2 + 2 \mathbf{x \cdot y} + \mathbf{|y|}^2
                                        + \mathbf{|x|}^2 - 2 \mathbf{x \cdot y} + \mathbf{|y|}^2 \\
                                      & = 2|\mathbf{x}|^2 + 2|\mathbf{y}|^2,
\end{align*}
meaning that the sum squared of the parallelogram's four sides equals
two times the sum squared of its two diagonals.

\hfill$\square$
